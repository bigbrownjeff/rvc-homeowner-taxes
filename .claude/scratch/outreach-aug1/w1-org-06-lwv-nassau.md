Dear League of Women Voters of Nassau County,

I am a homeowner in Rockville Centre. This is a courtesy note before a nonpartisan site I built goes public on August 1: rvc-taxes.jeffpinto.com. I wanted the League to see it first, because it is built on the values your work stands for, primary sources, plain explanation, and a clear path for residents to reach their own officials.

The site takes one hard local question, how a school district can keep cutting staff while enrollment falls, and traces it to public records. Rockville Centre passed its 2026-27 budget at $141,323,369 on May 19, cutting 22.2 teaching positions to stay under the tax cap, while enrollment has fallen from 3,533 students in 2015-16 to 3,276 and 2,076 owner-occupied homes, about 27.9 percent, are held by an owner aged 65 or older. Every figure links to its primary public source, and the site fact-checked its own earlier claims before publishing. It also includes a tool that looks up a resident's own state and county representatives and drafts a letter to them.

I understand the League reaches its positions through study and consensus, so I am not asking for an endorsement of any bill. I am asking two smaller things. Would you take a look before launch and tell me if anything reads as less than nonpartisan or not fully sourced, and if the League finds it a useful, even-handed resource, would you consider listing or sharing it as a voter-education tool on Nassau school finance.

Reply if you are open to either, and I would be glad to walk a member through the one-page deck. Nothing would be attributed to the League without your written sign-off.

Thank you for your service to Nassau voters.

Jeff Pinto

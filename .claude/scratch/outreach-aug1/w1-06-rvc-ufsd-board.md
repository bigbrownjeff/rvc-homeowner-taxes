Dear Members of the Board of Education,

I am a homeowner in the Rockville Centre school district. This is a courtesy note before a nonpartisan site I built goes public on August 1: rvc-taxes.jeffpinto.com. I wanted the Board to see it first, because it is on your side of the ledger.

The site starts from this year's budget, which the Board adopted at $141,323,369 and voters passed 1,915 to 1,195 on May 19, with 22.2 teaching positions and 40 teaching-assistant positions cut to stay under the tax cap. It sets that against enrollment, down from 3,533 students in 2015-16 to 3,276, and against the 2,076 owner-occupied homes here, about 27.9 percent, that have a homeowner aged 65 or older. The argument is that voluntary senior-to-family housing turnover is the least painful way to steady both enrollment and the levy. Every figure links to its primary public source.

The ask relevant to the Board concerns Section 467, the senior exemption. The maximum income limit localities may adopt for it rises to $75,000 on July 1, 2027, and new percentage tiers are already available. Before the district opts into any expansion, the site asks for a parcel-level fiscal note: how many parcels would qualify by tier, and what each option would shift onto non-exempt taxpayers. A guiding rule is that any senior who never moves should be left exactly as they are today; expansions should be paired with downsizing options so relief does not deepen the housing freeze.

One thing I would ask now: if it would help, I would welcome 15 minutes to walk the Board or the district's staff through the one-page deck. Reply and I will send times that work.

Thank you for your service to the district.

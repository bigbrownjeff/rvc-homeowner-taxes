Dear Ms. Finkel and AARP New York,

I am a homeowner in Rockville Centre. This is a courtesy note before a nonpartisan site I built goes public on August 1: rvc-taxes.jeffpinto.com. I wanted AARP New York to see it first, because the asks on it are meant to protect older homeowners, and I would rather you tell me if any of them read the wrong way.

The site connects two facts on Long Island. School districts are cutting staff to stay under the tax cap while enrollment falls, and a large share of homes are held by owners aged 65 or older. In Rockville Centre that is 2,076 owner-occupied homes, about 27.9 percent. Most older owners want to stay in the homes they know, and the site says so plainly. The point is narrower: for the minority who do want to downsize, the tax code adds a penalty, and removing it lets a larger home reach a younger family without anyone being pushed.

The two asks that touch your members are the ones I would most value your read on. First, the site backs S3309 and its Assembly companion A5288, which let a senior keep a property tax exemption in the year they move rather than losing it mid-year. S3309 cleared the Senate Aging committee 7 to 0 and then stalled in Finance. Second, on the Section 467 senior exemption, whose income limit rises to $75,000 on July 1, 2027, the site asks only for transparency before any local expansion, a parcel-level count of who qualifies, not a cut to anyone's relief. The rule I hold to throughout is that any senior who never moves is left exactly as they are today.

If AARP New York found this worth supporting, that would carry real weight, and I would be glad to have it. Reply if you are open to it and I will send a short paragraph you could approve for a "Backed by" note on the site. Nothing would be published without your written sign-off, and I would welcome 15 minutes to walk your advocacy staff through it first.

Thank you for your work for older New Yorkers.

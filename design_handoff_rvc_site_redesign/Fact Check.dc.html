01 · The big four: errors that would have been fatal in Albany
1 · "Exemptions cost the schools $6.9M a year": wrong kind of money

NY districts levy a fixed dollar amount. Exemptions shift the levy onto non-exempt neighbors; they do not reduce district revenue. And STAR, the largest piece of the claimed gap, is reimbursed by the State (RPTL §1306-a). The "recaptured school revenue" scenarios mislabeled taxpayer relief and state savings as district revenue. The brief rebuilds this math correctly, and the correct version is still persuasive.

2 · The §467 table was shifted $8,400, and outdated

The project claimed 50% off for incomes up to $58,399. Per Nassau's official brochure, 50% requires income ≤$50,000; $58,399 is the ceiling of the minimum 5% tier. Since Dec 2025 (Ch. 581) the scale runs 5-65%, and the statutory income cap rises to $75,000 on July 1, 2027. Implication: RVC's median senior homeowner (~$80K income) does not qualify for §467 at all, which broke the old "1,404 parcels × $4,900" arithmetic.

3 · "Prop 19-style portability for NY": solving a California problem New York does not have

California needs portability because Prop 13 taxes acquisition value. New York taxes current assessed value (RPTL §305): there is no basis to port, Nassau sales do not trigger reassessment, and a downsizing senior's taxes already fall with the cheaper home. The state centerpiece was rebuilt on NY-native vehicles: S3309/A5288, a deferral enabling act, and S3574.

4 · The story was stale: the deficit was already closed, by cutting classrooms

Early documents treated the $3.78M deficit as looming. It ended: the board adopted a $141.32M budget (+1.03%) on April 16, 2026, eliminating about 22 teaching positions and 40 teaching assistants, and voters passed it 1,915-1,195 on May 19. This is better ammunition than a projection: the cuts are what "do nothing about housing turnover" looks like.

02 · Framing fixes

Concepts, not just digits. What the project now says instead of what it used to say.

ReplaceWith
"Recaptured school tax revenue"; "deficit coverage"Burden-shift relief to other taxpayers plus state savings. The only channels that add district revenue from turnover: the tax-base growth factor, enrollment-linked aid, and budget-vote sustainability.
Enhanced STAR counted in district lossesSTAR is a state charge. A senior-to-family transition saves NY State about $2,058/yr (Enhanced $3,147 to Basic $1,089): the downsizing dividend that can fund the incentives.
"Seniors lose §467/STAR benefits if they move"Mostly myth: Enhanced STAR follows an eligible owner to any NY primary residence; §467 applies wherever adopted; Nassau waives its 12-month wait for prior holders. The real frictions: move-year timing (S3309 fixes it), capital gains, inventory.
"1,404 exempt senior parcels (18% of owner homes)"2,076 senior-owned homes (27.9%, ACS B25007), of which the §467-eligible subset is unknown until the county publishes parcel data. Never multiply every senior home by a full benefit stack.
"288 seats of spare capacity"A ~260-290 student enrollment decline (NYSED: 3,533 to 3,276). No public capacity study exists; say "empty seats" only in that bounded sense.
Marginal cost of a new student = $0One labeled assumption: low marginal cost while spare seats last, rising as they fill.

04 · What survives

The hypothesis holds; it just needed correct plumbing. The squeeze is real and now has a body count: 62 classroom positions cut to balance 2026-27, with revenue growing ~1% against +$2M in health insurance alone, and 61% of Nassau's 56 districts declining. The homes exist: 2,076 senior-owned, more than the project first assumed. The lock-in is real and fixable: §121 unindexed since 1997 with ~1.9M senior homes above the caps, benefit-timing traps that S3309 fixes, and missing downsize inventory.

And the corrected fiscal upside is attractive: each transition saves the State ~$2,058/yr in STAR, pays ~$3,300 in one-time transfer tax, relieves the §467 burden shift on neighbors, grows the levy cap through renovations, and refills empty seats. Every ask has a live vehicle and a named, current officeholder.

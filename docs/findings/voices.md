# Voices Library — LI Schools × Housing (compiled June 10, 2026)

## TASK B — Most Recent LI School Closures / Consolidations (2020–2026), Ranked

**Bottom line:** No Nassau district has actually closed a building in the 2020s. The most recent verified building closures on Long Island are Northport-East Northport's two elementary schools (2021, Suffolk). The most recent consolidation *decision* is Three Village's 2024 grade reconfiguration (implemented fall 2025). Nassau's closest brush was Long Beach's East Elementary, proposed for closure and saved by parent pressure in January 2025 — which doubles as the deck's "it almost happened here" hook.

### #1 — Northport-East Northport UFSD (Suffolk): closed TWO elementary schools, 2021
*The modern replacement for the stale 1976 Levittown reference.*
- **Schools closed:** Dickinson Avenue Elementary and Bellerose Avenue Elementary (both East Northport).
- **Decision:** Board of Education voted **5–2 on April 29, 2021** to adopt "Adapted Scenario A," denying a community request to pause a year; schools closed **effective Aug. 31, 2021**, with the district reorganized K-4 / 5-8 / 9-12 for 2021-22.
- **Stated reasons (explicitly enrollment + budget):** district enrollment fell from 5,748 (2014-15) to 5,138 (2019-20), **−10.6% in six years**; the Sept. 2020 LIPA Northport-plant tax settlement steps LIPA's payments down from **$86M to $46M over seven years**. Estimated savings: **~$7M/year**.
- **Closure-vote voices (2021 — context only, outside the 2023-26 quote window):**
  - Tammie Topel (community member/BOE candidate): "The community needs time to recover, our kids need time to recover. And we're not allowing that time by shutting down two schools."
  - Katherine Epanchin-Butuc (parent of three): "This is a bitter pill to swallow."
  - Trustee Victoria Buscareno (voted no): "I'm listening to important things and the conversations that are going on, and our community is asking us to pause."
  - Supt. Robert Banzer: "Pausing does not eliminate the burden, it just shifts it for another year."
  - Trustee David Stein (voted yes): "My decision tonight is not easy, but I know it is right beyond myself and my own personal interests."
- **Sources:**

  - Patch, "2 East Northport Elementary Schools To Close Following Vote": https://patch.com/new-york/northport/2-east-northport-elementary-schools-close-following-vote
  - Daily Voice, "Long Island District To Close Two Schools During Summer" (May 2021): https://dailyvoice.com/new-york/nassau/schools/long-island-district-to-close-two-schools-during-summer/808033/

### #2 — Three Village CSD (Suffolk): district-wide grade consolidation, voted Jan 2024, effective fall 2025
*The most recent consolidation decision on LI — buildings stayed open, grades consolidated.*
- **Decision:** BOE voted at its **Jan. 10, 2024** meeting (over public comments urging delay) to move 6th grade up into the junior highs (creating 6-8 middle schools) and 9th grade into Ward Melville HS (4-year high school), **implemented fall 2025** (2025-26).
- **Stated reasons:** improve **financial stability**, realign the district amid **declining enrollment** (~7,000 students in 2012-13 to ~5,433 in 2025 — a **23% drop**; 1,296 fewer than 2015), match state/national grade norms; cited ~**$450K/year** transportation savings alone. Supt. Kevin Scanlon repeatedly stressed no decision to close/repurpose any of the five elementary schools — yet.
- **Sources:**
  - TBR News Media, "Sixth and ninth grades to move up in Three Village School District" (Jan 19, 2024): https://tbrnewsmedia.com/sixth-and-ninth-grades-to-move-up-in-three-village-school-district/
  - TBR News Media, "Three Village school board to vote on district reconfiguration in January": https://tbrnewsmedia.com/three-village-school-board-to-vote-on-district-reconfiguration-in-january/
  - TBR News Media, "Three Village school district eyes school restructuring, seeks community input": https://tbrnewsmedia.com/three-village-school-district-eyes-school-restructuring-seeks-community-input/

### #3 — Long Beach UFSD (Nassau): East Elementary closure proposed, defeated Jan. 27, 2025
*Nassau's closest 2020s brush with an actual closure — proof the endgame has reached Nassau.*
- **Decision:** After ~a year of closure study (first triggered by feared Foundation Aid cuts, then justified by declining enrollment), the board announced **Jan. 27, 2025** it would keep East Elementary open "for the foreseeable future"; a 4,000+ signature petition and packed meetings drove the reversal. District pivoted to ~$6M in HVAC upgrades for the 98-year-old building.
- **Sources:**
  - News 12 (Jan 14-15, 2025): https://longisland.news12.com/2025/01/15/parents-challenge-long-beach-school-district-over-potential-elementary-school-closure/5JkcWyw2Fu8BNaCJFdUwhK
  - News 12, "Parents celebrate decision to keep East Elementary School open" (Jan 27, 2025): https://longisland.news12.com/long-beach-school-board-to-discuss-future-of-east-elementary-school
  - Patch (Jan 28, 2025): https://patch.com/new-york/longbeach/school-board-votes-keep-east-elementary-open-foreseeable-future

**Nassau fiscal-cliff companion (cuts, not closure):** North Shore CSD (Glen Head/Sea Cliff — Nassau) lost its biggest taxpayer to the 2022 LIPA Glenwood Landing settlement; faces **>$8M revenue loss by 2027-28**, homeowners' tax-class share already up from ~70% to >80%; adopted an austere $125M budget for 2025-26. Sources: Long Island Press (Apr 29, 2025) https://www.longislandpress.com/2025/04/29/north-shore-school-district-adopts-125m-budget/ ; (Mar 3, 2025) https://www.longislandpress.com/2025/03/03/bes-concerns-north-shore-budget/

**Dead ends checked (no 2020s closures found):** Sachem (closed 3 schools in 2016; 2024-25 answered its crisis with ~91 job cuts + failed-then-passed budget, no closures), Smithtown, Comsewogue, Mount Sinai, Port Jefferson (debate only), Lindenhurst, Copiague, Wantagh, Malverne, Bellmore, West Hempstead, Lawrence, Amityville, Levittown, Massapequa, Baldwin. North Fork merger talk (Southold/Mattituck/Greenport) stalled — districts opted out of the state regionalization plan in Jan 2025 (Suffolk Times: https://suffolktimes.timesreview.com/2025/01/most-area-schools-opt-out-of-state-regionalization-plan/).

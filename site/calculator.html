Per-Pupil Spending Comparison

ROI Assessment: RVC spends $40.2K per student (2023-24 ST-3 actuals; NYSED's official per-pupil measure is $37,973) — among Long Island's higher spenders, comparable to Jericho ($40.9K) and well above Garden City ($32.7K) and Manhasset ($35.1K). The district delivers math proficiency 55% above the state average and reading 41% above (2021-22 NYSED data via aggregators) — a strong return, but the spending level is significant. The 2025-26 budget is $139.89M with a 2.6% tax levy increase. Verified reserve components: unappropriated fund balance $4.8M (3.5% of budget; 4% statutory max), appropriated fund balance falling from $2.8M to ~$1.6M, plus ~$4.15M in ERS/TRS reserves. Board-analysis estimates (M.B. Joyce) put unrestricted cash at ~$8M vs. a ~14% peer average and $27M at Garden City (GC's 2025-26 budget: $137.8M).
2026-27 Budget — resolved (spring 2026): The January-projected $3.78M deficit was closed: the board adopted a $141.32M budget (+1.03%) on April 16, 2026 — eliminating ≈22 teaching and 40 teaching-assistant positions — and voters approved it 1,915–1,195 on May 19, 2026. The levy rises 2.06%, under the final ~2.1% cap. Structural drivers remain: health insurance +10% (~$2M), out-of-district special-ed placements projected at $6.38M, and total revenue growth of only ~1% (~$1.4M). Projected enrollment: 3,260.

Monthly Electric: RVC vs. PSEG LI

Municipal Electric Advantage: RVC's Electric Dept. provides power at $0.108–$0.114/kWh (supply) vs. PSEG Long Island's higher rates — saving the average household at least ~$200–$350/year (all-in gaps can exceed $400). About 75% of RVC's power comes from NYPA Niagara Falls hydroelectric, making it cleaner and cheaper.
Volunteer Fire Department: RVC's fire protection is provided by ~340 volunteers across 5 stations, avoiding the payroll of a professional paid department — unofficial estimates put the avoided cost at $8–12M annually (no published study). This is one of the village's most significant cost efficiencies.

NYS Medicaid covers approximately one-third of all New Yorkers. The program accounts for the largest single expense in the state budget when including the federal matching funds. NYS has one of the most generous Medicaid programs in the nation, covering dental, vision, long-term care, and behavioral health — benefits many other states do not offer. The state share is funded by income tax and a dedicated health care surcharge.
Nassau County Impact: A substantial share of Nassau residents (est. 15–20%) are Medicaid enrollees. The state assumed growth of the counties' Medicaid share starting with the 2012-13 state budget (fully phased in by 2015), insulating Nassau's property-tax levy from Medicaid inflation; precise county-level savings figures are unofficial.

NYS NAEP Scores vs. National Avg

Per-Pupil Spending: NYS vs. Nation

Performance Paradox: New York spends the most per student in the nation ($31,918 vs. $17,619 national average — 81% above, Census FY2024), yet its 2024 NAEP scores sit at or below the national average in three of four cells (4th-grade math 234 vs 237; 8th-grade reading 257 vs 257; 8th-grade math 271 vs 272), edging the nation only in 4th-grade reading (215 vs 214). The gap between spending leadership and average outcomes is one of the most debated issues in NYS education policy.
What RVC Gets: RVC UFSD receives modest Foundation Aid (the formula favors higher-need districts), but residents benefit from STAR — $1,089 Basic / $3,147 Enhanced credit (DTF 2025 final, Class 1; pre-2015 exemption holders: $1,068 / $2,856) — and the district participates in BOCES shared services that reduce per-pupil administrative costs.

Medicare Part A (hospital insurance) covers inpatient stays, skilled nursing, and hospice for those 65+. Part B covers doctor visits and outpatient services. Part D covers prescription drugs. The Medicare Advantage (Part C) program now covers ~54% of eligible beneficiaries (2025), up from 13% in 2005.

RVC Effective Property Tax Rate: Same Verified Bill, Three Value Bases
One verified in-village bill (~$21.1K total incl. the village line; Nassau LRV parcel tax tables + village rate, July 2026) divided by three different value bases gives three different "effective rates": 3.23% of the bill-era taxable EMV ($653K), 2.85% of the county's frozen Fair Market Value ($741K), and ~2.6% of a current-market estimate ($819K median). There is no single "RVC effective rate" — quote the basis with the number, and treat the calculator's 2.57% as a planning mid-point of a ~2.0–3.2% range depending on basis and parcel.

Dear Legislator Davis,

I am a homeowner in Rockville Centre and one of your constituents in Legislative District 1, which covers all of the village. This is a courtesy note before a nonpartisan site I built goes public on August 1: rvc-taxes.jeffpinto.com. I wanted your office to see it first.

The site connects two facts. Our school district passed its 2026-27 budget at $141,323,369 on May 19, cutting 22.2 teaching positions and 40 teaching-assistant positions to stay under the tax cap. At the same time, 2,076 of our owner-occupied homes, about 27.9 percent, have a homeowner aged 65 or older, and enrollment has fallen from 3,533 students in 2015-16 to 3,276. Every figure links to its primary public source on a facts page.

The county ask relevant to your seat is a data request. No one outside the Assessment Department can say how many parcels hold the Section 467 senior exemption in any school district, or what expanding it would shift onto other taxpayers. As I understand it, the maximum income limit localities may adopt for that exemption rises to $75,000 on July 1, 2027, and every school board and village board weighing that decision needs the count. The site asks that you direct the Department of Assessment to publish Section 467 and Enhanced STAR parcel counts by school district and tier, every year.

One thing I would ask now: if it would help, I would welcome 15 minutes to walk you or your staff through the one-page deck before launch. Reply and I will send times that work for your office.

Thank you for your time.

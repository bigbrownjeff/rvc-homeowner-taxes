# Fact-Check: Long Island School District Claims
Checked 2026-06-09. Primary source: NYSED data site (data.nysed.gov), 2024-25 BEDS-day enrollment (latest published — `year=2026` still redirects to 2024-25 as of today). Budgets: 2025-26 voter-approved plans.

## 1. Hewlett-Woodmere UFSD — cuts/enrollment
- **VERDICT: mostly CONFIRMED; "3,100+ total enrollment" WRONG.**
- "At least 11 teaching positions" cut for 2025-26 due to declining enrollment — CONFIRMED. Announced at Feb 5, 2025 BOE budget session. ([LI Herald, Feb 2025](https://www.liherald.com/eastrockaway/stories/hewlett-woodmere-to-cut-teaching-positions,213353))
- Projected elementary decline of ~200 students vs 2022-23 — CONFIRMED (same article).
- Total enrollment "3,100+" — WRONG. NYSED K-12: **2,650** (2024-25); 2,854 (2019-20); even 2015-16 was only 2,938. District hasn't had 3,100+ K-12 in a decade. ([NYSED](https://data.nysed.gov/enrollment.php?year=2025&instid=800000049523))

## 2. Locust Valley CSD
- **VERDICT: CONFIRMED.**
- 2,034 (2018-19) → ~1,818 (2024-25), projected ~1,725 by 2027-28 — CONFIRMED as district/Herald-reported figures. Supt. **Kristen Turnow** said "This is a big deal" and that she found **only five houses on the market under $1M** in the district ("this is a Long Island problem; this is not a Locust Valley problem"). ([LI Herald, Apr 2025](https://www.liherald.com/stories/school-districts-across-the-north-shore-address-nassau-county-public-schools-lowering-enrollment,214116); [LI Press, Feb 13 2025](https://www.longislandpress.com/2025/02/13/locust-valley-enrollment/))
- Caveat: NYSED BEDS-day K-12 for 2024-25 is **1,754** ([NYSED](https://data.nysed.gov/enrollment.php?year=2025&instid=800000048920)) — the 1,818 is the district's own count (likely incl. pre-K/different date). Direction and magnitude check out.

## 3. Levittown UFSD — "two schools closed"
- **VERDICT: WRONG as implied / OUTDATED by ~50 years.**
- Pintail Lane Primary and Cherrywood Elementary **did exist and did close for enrollment reasons — in 1976 and the mid-1970s**, respectively. Pintail Lane was demolished (lot vacant); Cherrywood was razed in the late '70s for townhouses. **No Levittown school closures in 2024-26.** ([Wikipedia: Levittown UFSD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levittown_Union_Free_School_District))
- Current elementary schools (6): **Abbey Lane, East Broadway, Gardiners Avenue, Lee Road, Northside, Summit Lane** (plus 2 middle, 2 high schools).
- 7,177 students — OUTDATED: that's 2022-23. NYSED 2024-25 K-12: **7,061**. ([NYSED](https://data.nysed.gov/enrollment.php?year=2025&instid=800000049713))
- "$233.8M budget/revenue" — OUTDATED/MISLABELED: matches US News/NCES "annual revenue $233,765,000" (circa FY2021-22 federal finance data). Voter-approved **2025-26 budget = $274.1M** (+4.8%); proposed 2026-27 = $289.3M. ([LI Herald](https://www.liherald.com/seaford/stories/levittown-voters-approve-2741-million-school-budget-for-2025-26,215111); [US News](https://www.usnews.com/education/k12/new-york/districts/levittown-union-free-school-district-102820))

## 4. Long Beach City SD — East Elementary
- **VERDICT: debate CONFIRMED; outcome = SCHOOL STAYED OPEN; "~2,800 students" WRONG.**
- Year-long closure debate CONFIRMED; parents publicly challenged the district (News 12, Jan 15, 2025); talks were renewed after survey results; board ultimately announced **East Elementary remains open** (board president Sam Pinto: "I listened to the tremendous advocacy from the community"; trustee Dennis Ryan dissented over the 98-year-old building). ([News 12](https://longisland.news12.com/parents-challenge-long-beach-school-district-over-potential-elementary-school-closure); [News 12 — decision](https://longisland.news12.com/long-beach-school-board-to-discuss-future-of-east-elementary-school); [LI Herald](https://www.liherald.com/stories/east-elementary-school-remains-open-budget-updates,212809))
- Enrollment: NYSED 2024-25 K-12 = **3,265**, not ~2,800. ([NYSED](https://data.nysed.gov/enrollment.php?year=2025&instid=800000049221))

## 5. Oyster Bay-East Norwich CSD
- **VERDICT: CLOSE (figures), CONFIRMED (Ianni/pre-K, §467).**
- Claimed 1,585 (2015) → 1,347 (2025), ~15%. NYSED: **1,569 (2015-16) → 1,310 (2024-25) = -16.5%**. Claim's figures are slightly different (district counts) but decline magnitude is right. ([NYSED 2024-25](https://data.nysed.gov/enrollment.php?year=2025&instid=800000048868); [NYSED 2015-16](https://data.nysed.gov/enrollment.php?year=2016&instid=800000048868))
- Supt. **Dr. Francesco Ianni** credits universal Pre-K (plus rankings gains) for OBEN's relative stabilization — CONFIRMED. ([LI Herald, Apr 2025/Apr 2026](https://www.liherald.com/stories/north-shore-public-school-districts-leaders-address-nassau-countys-low-enrollment-issue,214124))
- Town of Oyster Bay has adopted the RPTL **§467** senior citizens exemption (sliding 5-50% for 65+, income ≤ $58,399) — CONFIRMED, though it is long-standing, not a recent adoption. ([Town of Oyster Bay](https://oysterbaytown.com/departments/receiver-of-taxes/tax-exemptions/); [Town Code Art. I](https://ecode360.com/26877745))

## Most important corrections
1. **Levittown school closures (claim 3): the two named schools closed in 1976/mid-1970s, not recently.** Presenting Pintail Lane and Cherrywood as current/recent closures is the most misleading item on the list. Levittown still runs 6 elementary schools and closed nothing in 2024-26; its budget is $274.1M (2025-26), not $233.8M.
2. **Hempstead ~7,000 is off by ~2,000 students** — NYSED 2024-25 K-12 is 5,071. Largest absolute error in the set.
3. **Baldwin ~5,500 is off by ~1,200** (actual 4,309) and **Island Trees ~3,000 is off by ~740** (actual 2,261) — both materially overstate enrollment.
4. **Long Beach: East Elementary did NOT close** — the board kept it open (spring 2025) after community advocacy; and the district has ~3,265 students, not ~2,800. Any narrative implying closure or smaller scale is wrong.
5. **Garden City's 2025-26 budget is $137.8M, not $122.7M** (the claimed figure resembles the tax levy, not total spending); Roosevelt's enrollment is *rising* (3,020), undercutting it as a "decline" example.

Dear Senator Bynoe,

I am a homeowner in Rockville Centre and one of your constituents in Senate District 6. This is a courtesy note before a nonpartisan site I built goes public on August 1: rvc-taxes.jeffpinto.com. I wanted your office to see it first, in part because of your own record on housing.

The site connects two facts. Our school district passed its 2026-27 budget at $141,323,369 on May 19, cutting 22.2 teaching positions and 40 teaching-assistant positions to stay under the tax cap. At the same time, 2,076 of our owner-occupied homes, about 27.9 percent, have a homeowner aged 65 or older, and enrollment has fallen from 3,533 students in 2015-16 to 3,276. When an older owner who wants to downsize can move without penalty, a larger home opens up for a younger family. Every figure on the site links to its primary public source on a facts page.

The state ask relevant to your seat is S3309, which lets a senior keep a property tax exemption in the year they move. It cleared the Senate Aging committee 7 to 0 on April 21 and then stalled in Finance. The site asks that both S3309 and its Assembly companion A5288 be advanced in 2027. It removes a real penalty on downsizing at almost no cost.

One thing I would ask now: if it would help, I would welcome 15 minutes to walk you or your staff through the one-page deck before launch. Reply and I will send times that work for your office.

Thank you for your time.

# Adjudicator's Brief Plan — RVC / Nassau Homeowner Tax + Desegregation Co-Benefit

**Verdict: GENUINE TIE → TWO briefs.** The dollar axis and the equity axis diverge in lever,
audience, and ask. They cannot be honestly collapsed (the dollar levers are segregation-neutral;
the desegregation levers cost the named RVC homeowner money). One brief for a named homeowner +
Town-of-Hempstead audience; one for the policy/coalition audience pursuing desegregation. Manufacturing
a single brief would force the same "erasing racism cuts your bill" lie all four critics flag as false.

But BOTH briefs are re-built from the critiques, not the lead memo, because the critiques are right
where they overlap and I verified their load-bearing claims.

## BRIEF 1 — "Cut the Bill You Actually Pay" (DOLLAR-maximizing)

- **Audience:** the named RVC homeowner + Rockville Centre / Town-of-Hempstead homeowners and a reform
  village trustee. Split incidence stated up front (RVC vs. unincorporated ToH).
- **Thesis:** The biggest, most certain dollars on an RVC bill are personal and administrative, not
  structural — claimed exemptions (STAR/§467), a grievance on the frozen roll, double-assessment
  cleanup, and measured BOCES back-office trims. The splashy structural "savings" are one-time,
  diffuse to the county, or land on a different parcel. For unincorporated ToH only, the commissioner
  sanitation takeover is a real mid-hundreds-per-year lever — sequenced after a sunlight campaign.
- **Lead numbers:** Enhanced STAR $2,856/yr (Basic $1,068), state-funded (RPTL §1306-a); §467 up to
  65% off county+town+school (Ch. 581/2025), ceiling income $58,399; sixth frozen roll → permanent
  grievance AV cut; special districts 51.8% of town revenue / $946 per household / Hempstead bills up
  to 3x town-run / ~$18M-$35.7M county-wide recoverable (ToH only); BOCES aid 36-90%, $30K→$60K cap
  (2025), ~$80-160M aggregate potential but treat as 1-2% with measurement teeth.
- **Asks:** (1) File/verify STAR + §467 (confirm RVC UFSD's adopted 65% tier with district clerk);
  (2) grieve the frozen assessment Jan-Mar; (3) village board adopts county roll (RPTL §1402);
  (4) districts move back-office into Nassau BOCES/CWSSI with audited savings clauses; (5) ToH only:
  sunlight mandates (audits, November commissioner elections, contract posting) THEN takeover referendum.
- **Equity framing:** segregation-neutral by construction; the one quiet convergence is that a fresher,
  accurate roll corrects documented regressive over-assessment (Berry; Nassau commercial-skewed refunds)
  — a funding-equity byproduct, not a desegregation claim. Do NOT tell this homeowner erasing racism
  cuts their bill.
- **Why over alternatives:** ranked on real/recurring/named-bill dollars. Merger and pooling are
  zero-or-negative for RVC; county-guarantee reform is diffuse pennies; sanitation has zero RVC incidence.

## BRIEF 2 — "Erase the Lines, Honestly" (EQUITY-maximizing desegregation)

- **Audience:** ERASE Racism-aligned advocates, fair-housing litigators, BOCES/regional policymakers,
  state legislators — NOT the wealthy-district homeowner (for whom this is a cost).
- **Thesis:** LI's ~56-district map is an adjudicated segregation machine. But the durable desegregation
  lever is the student-ASSIGNMENT rule + the HOUSING root cause — not the structural container.
  Pooling and merger move dollars, not children, and re-segregate the moment the assignment rule is
  removed (Wake, Twin Cities). Lead with housing enforcement and BOCES regional magnets / controlled
  choice; treat tax-base pooling + assessment-accuracy as the funding floor (necessary, insufficient).
- **Lead numbers:** LI top-10 most-segregated US region; intensely-segregated (90-100% nonwhite)
  districts more than doubled 2004-2016, ~$10K less revenue/student (ERASE Racism); EdBuild Fault Lines
  ~$4,200 cross-border gap "largely because of … local property taxes"; Newsday 40% disparate treatment
  (2019); Garden City 2d Cir. 2016 adjudicated intentional discrimination; Twin Cities 6→100 segregated
  schools 1995-2015 under pooling (the cautionary number).
- **Asks:** (1) Fund/site fair-share affordable housing in exclusionary white districts + Newsday-grade
  anti-steering enforcement + continue the Nassau steering litigation; (2) stand up BOCES regional
  magnet / inter-district controlled-choice enrollment with a binding diversity-controlled assignment
  rule; (3) route freed BOCES back-office dollars to high-need districts to chip the $10K gap WITHOUT a
  boundary change; (4) pursue commercial tax-base pooling + assessment-accuracy as a funding floor,
  labeled insufficient; (5) reserve inclusive cross-line merger ("5 Rs," minority-community-led, no
  screened tracks, grade-banding over forced relocation) for willing communities only.
- **Equity framing:** contested remedy, stated honestly. Consolidation can integrate (Wake held while
  the assignment policy lived) OR harm — dollar-driven mergers close under-enrolled minority schools
  first (P.S. 305 takeover, murals painted over, lost Black/Latino local control). The 73% pooling poll
  is a tell that it doesn't threaten composition. The cost lands on the wealthy white district; that is
  the engine of every failed LI vote. Cite ERASE Racism / Newsday / EdBuild / NYC Comptroller.
- **Why over alternatives:** the verified Wake/Twin Cities natural experiments show the container without
  the assignment rule does not desegregate; pure dollar-equalization is separate-but-more-equal. Merger
  is generationally infeasible (every LI vote failed) and double-edged.

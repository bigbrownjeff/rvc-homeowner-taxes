Dear Senator Canzoneri-Fitzpatrick,

I live in Rockville Centre, as you do, and I am writing because your bill S3309 sits at the center of a nonpartisan site I built that goes public on August 1: rvc-taxes.jeffpinto.com. I wanted you to see it first.

The site connects two facts. Our school district passed its 2026-27 budget at $141,323,369 on May 19, cutting 22.2 teaching positions and 40 teaching-assistant positions to stay under the tax cap. At the same time, 2,076 of our owner-occupied homes, about 27.9 percent, have a homeowner aged 65 or older, and enrollment has fallen from 3,533 students in 2015-16 to 3,276. When an older owner who wants to downsize can move without penalty, a larger home opens up for a younger family. Every figure links to its primary public source on a facts page.

S3309, your bill, lets a senior keep a property tax exemption in the year they move. It cleared the Senate Aging committee 7 to 0 on April 21 and then stalled in Finance, and its Assembly companion A5288 never left Real Property Taxation. The site thanks you for sponsoring it and asks that both bills advance in 2027. It removes a real penalty on downsizing at almost no cost.

One thing I would ask now: if it would help, I would welcome 15 minutes to walk you or your staff through the one-page deck before launch. Reply and I will send times that work for you.

Thank you for carrying this bill.

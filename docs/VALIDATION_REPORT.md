# Validation Report — RVC Homeowner Taxes Project
**Date:** June 9, 2026 · **Scope:** every factual claim, figure, and calculation in `RVC_Housing_Transition_Action_Plan.docx`, `RVC_Senior_Transition_Financial_Model.xlsx`, and `RVC_Tax_Calculator.html`, validated against primary public sources.
**Receipts:** per-claim verdicts with URLs live in [`docs/findings/`](findings/) (school-finance, exemptions, demographics, politics, coalition, calculator-macro, internal-consistency).

---

## 1. Bottom line

The project's **thesis survives validation; its arithmetic and framing do not.** Of ~120 checked claims: roughly half are confirmed, a quarter are outdated or imprecise, and a quarter are wrong — including four errors that would be fatal in front of a legislative audience:

1. **The "$6.9M revenue gap / $688K–$3.4M recapture" headline is triply wrong.** (a) NY school districts levy a *fixed dollar amount* — exemptions shift burden onto other taxpayers; they do not reduce district revenue (OSC: "Every exemption granted… shifts the tax burden to the non-exempt properties"). (b) STAR is *state-funded* (RPTL §1306-a) — the $2,680-per-parcel Enhanced STAR component of the "gap" costs the district nothing. (c) The 1,404-parcel base multiplies *all* senior-owned homes by a benefit stack only a low-income minority can receive (§467 requires income ≤ $58,399; RVC's senior median income is ~$77–80K).
2. **The §467 tier table is shifted up by $8,400 and outdated.** 50% requires income ≤ $50,000; $58,399 is the ceiling for the *minimum* 5% tier; the "$66,799" tier does not exist. And since Ch. 581 of 2025 (signed Dec 5, 2025), Nassau's scale runs **5–65%** (65% ≤ $47,000), with the statutory income cap rising to **$75,000 on July 1, 2027**. *[Note 2026-07-18: these are two separate 2025 changes — Ch. 581 (A.3698-A) created only the 65/60/55% tiers; the $75K cap is a distinct amendment verified in the statute text of §467(3)(a), enacting chapter unidentified. See REFRESH_2026-07.md.]*
3. **"Prop 19-style portability" is a category error in New York.** CA needs portability because Prop 13 taxes acquisition value. NY taxes current assessed value (RPTL §305) — there is no basis to port, Nassau sales don't trigger reassessment, and a downsizing senior's taxes already fall with the cheaper home. The state-level centerpiece must be rebuilt on NY-native tools (see §5).
4. **The story is stale: the deficit is closed.** The 2026-27 budget ($141,323,369, +1.03%, levy +2.06% under a ~2.1% final cap) was adopted Apr 16, 2026 and **passed by voters 1,915–1,195 on May 19, 2026** — balanced by cutting ~22 teaching positions and ~40 TAs. The deficit is no longer a projection to warn about; the classroom cuts are the proof of the structural problem. (Mary Beth Joyce, the cited analyst, won a board seat in that same election.)

## 3. What was fabricated, misattributed, or unverifiable

**Effectively false (fix before any external use):**
- §467 income tiers ($58,399→50%, scale to $66,799) — no such tiers exist anywhere in NY law. The real Nassau table (county brochure Rev. 3-26): 65% ≤ $47,000 … 5% at $57,500–58,399.
- Levittown's "two schools closed due to declining population" as current evidence — Pintail Lane closed **1976**, Cherrywood mid-1970s. No Nassau-relevant closure occurred 2024-26; Long Beach's East Elementary **stayed open** (spring 2025) after parent advocacy.
- "Sen. Todd Kaminsky (SD-9)" (resigned July 2022; now **Patricia Canzoneri-Fitzpatrick, R — an RVC resident**), "Asm. Ed Ra (AD-19)" (RVC is **AD-21, Judy Griffin (D)**), "Senate Majority Leader Schumer" (Minority Leader; Thune holds the majority).
- Itemization rates (68% RVC / 62% Nassau / 35% NY / 13% US) — pre-TCJA magnitudes; IRS SOI TY2022: **25.7% (ZIP 11570), ~19.8% (Nassau proxy), 10.4% (NY), 9.6% (US)**.
- STAR savings $1,220/$2,680 — match no DTF row for Rockville Centre (Class 1 2025 final: exemption $1,068/$2,856; credit $1,089/$3,147.01).
- Homeownership 78% / ~7,800 owner-occupied homes — ACS: **74.5% / 7,453** (no vintage shows 78%).
- NAEP 2024: claimed NY 8th-grade advantages (266 vs 259 reading; 279 vs 274 math) don't exist — actual NY 215/234/257/271 vs national 214/237/257/272 (at/below national in 3 of 4 cells); the cited state "ranks" aren't published by NAEP at all. Ironically the *true* numbers make the spending-vs-results paradox stronger.
- "$7.6B Local Gov't Aid (AIM)" — AIM is **$715.2M/yr** (OSC), a 10× error. "Title I ≈ $3,700 per disadvantaged student to RVC" — actual 2025-26 allocation is **$126,911 total** (~0.09% of budget). LIRR Zone 4 monthly is **$264.25** (2026), not ~$349; LIRR carried **75.5M** riders in 2024, not 89.3M. RVC no longer operates its own sewage treatment plant (one of six districts pumping to Nassau's Bay Park/South Shore facility).

**Misattributed:**
- "Boomers own 28% of large homes vs millennials-with-kids 14%" is **Redfin (Jan 2024: 28.2%/14.2%; 2026 refresh: 28%/16%)**, not the Minneapolis Fed. The Minneapolis Fed piece (Horwich, Nov 14, 2024, "How higher property taxes increase home affordability") is real and separately useful — it argues property taxes *reduce* lock-in and shift ownership toward younger families.
- Garden City "$122.7M budget" — that's GC's 2023-24 ST-3 *actual spend* (per-pupil basis of the substack); GC's 2025-26 *budget* is **$137.8M**. Its "$27M reserves" exists only in the substack analysis — not independently confirmed.

**Unverifiable as published (label as estimates or get the data):**
- $15,230 average total tax on the median home, and the 62/18/12/3/3/2 bill split — plausible, but no public source; the cited Ownwell page actually shows $1,465/0.22% (village-levy-only, unusable). The 2.073% "effective rate" is just $15,230 ÷ $735,000.
  - **[Corrected 2026-07-18, math audit]** Resolved the other way: $15,230.09 is a *verified school+library-only* bill (Nassau LRV, in-village parcel, AV 653) — this report's re-label of it as "the total" was itself wrong; the model's original "school tax" label was right. A typical in-village *total* is ≈$21.1K, and the 2.073% rate understated real totals ~19%. See `docs/MATH-AUDIT-2026-07.md` and `site/reconcile.html`.
- The count of RVC parcels actually receiving §467 / Enhanced STAR — knowable only via the assessment roll / DTF exemption reports / FOIL (the plan's own Phase-1 ask). Until then, every dollar figure built on "1,404 exempt parcels × $4,900" is conjecture.
- "288 seats of spare capacity" (it's an enrollment *decline*, no facilities study exists), Riverside's "+$15K/student" (a resident's public comment), "~$22M state aid" (published: $19.8M in 2024-25, "15% of budget"), "$15K/family local retail spending," "20–40 parcels per §467 tier."

## 5. What the validated record actually supports (the corrected argument)

The hypothesis — *accelerating senior-to-family housing turnover is the least-painful way to sustain RVC's school infrastructure* — stands on five verified legs, none of which need the broken "recapture" math:

1. **The squeeze is real and now has a body count.** Enrollment 3,533 → 3,276 (NYSED, 2015-16 → 2024-25; district counts: 3,548 → 3,260 projected); revenue growth ~1% vs health insurance alone +$2M; reserves thin ($4.8M unappropriated, 3.5%, vs 4% statutory max); 2026-27 balanced only by cutting ~22 teachers + 40 TAs. 61% of Nassau's 56 districts share the decline (Nassau BOCES via LI Herald).
2. **The homes exist and are demographically locked.** 2,076 RVC owner-households (27.9%) are headed by someone 65+ (ACS B25007) — more, not fewer, than the project assumed. Empty-nesters hold large homes at ~2× the rate of millennial families (Redfin). Locust Valley's superintendent found five sub-$1M listings district-wide — the family pipeline is inventory-starved.
3. **Lock-in has identifiable, removable causes.** Federal: §121 exclusion ($250K/$500K) unindexed since 1997 — AEI: ~1.9M senior-owned homes sit above it ($620B unrealized gains); a fix exists (H.R. 1340 / S. 3332). State: benefit-continuity friction in the year of a move (S3309/A5288 fixes it); fear of losing §467/Enhanced STAR is mostly misinformation — both follow an eligible senior to a new NY home. Academic: age-based property-tax exemptions measurably *increase* senior retention in place (Banzhaf et al., NBER w25468) — RVC's growing senior tax stack (65% tier now legal; $75K income cap in 2027) deepens lock-in unless paired with mobility incentives.
4. **The honest fiscal upside is still attractive — just relabel the beneficiaries.** Each senior-to-family transition: (a) *shifts burden back* — the parcel's §467 exemption ends, lowering everyone else's rate (relief to taxpayers, not district revenue); (b) *saves the state* ~$2,058/yr (Enhanced credit $3,147 → Basic $1,089) — a "downsizing dividend" Albany can recycle into incentives; (c) *pays the state* ~$3,300 one-time transfer tax (0.4% on ~$820K); (d) *grows the levy cap* — buyer renovations add physical value, which raises the allowable levy via the tax-base growth factor (the only lawful way turnover adds district revenue); (e) *adds enrollment-linked aid* and budget-vote support. New students land in a district that has shed ~270 since 2016 at schools like Riverside (173 → 135).
5. **Every lever has a live vehicle.** Federal: H.R. 1340 (Panetta/Kelly; 117+ cosponsors — **Gillen not yet among them**; she's an RVC resident with a SALT-relief record and a June 23, 2026 primary). State: S3309/A5288 (exemption portability in a move year — reported from Aging 7-0, Apr 2026), S3574 (renewal-notice protection), plus a NY senior school-tax **deferral** enabling act modeled on OR/MA/WA/TX (6%/8%/5%/5% interest, lien repaid at sale). County: parcel-level §467/STAR transparency before opting into the 65% tier or the 2027 $75K cap. Local: fiscal notes on every exemption expansion; downsizing pilot; ADU/right-size supply.

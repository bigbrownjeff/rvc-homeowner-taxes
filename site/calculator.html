South Side High School ranks #611 nationally (U.S. News). The district spends $40.2K per student on ST-3 actuals, among Long Island's higher spenders, and delivers strong outcomes for it.

2026-27, resolved: the January $3.78M projected deficit was closed by the April 16 adopted budget ($141.32M, +1.03%), eliminating about 22 teaching and 40 teaching-assistant positions. Voters approved it 1,915-1,195 on May 19. Structural drivers remain: health insurance +10%, out-of-district special ed at $6.38M, revenue growth ~1%. This squeeze is why the housing turnover agenda exists.

Policing cost split (corrected July 2026): because RVC runs its own police department, in-village parcels are exempt from the Nassau police-district tax and pay only the county police headquarters line. Village policing is funded through the village levy instead.

The data ask lives here: the Assessment Department holds the parcel-level senior exemption counts that every downstream fiscal decision needs. It does not publish them by school district.

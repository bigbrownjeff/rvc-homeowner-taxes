The hypothesis this project has tested since March 2026: **accelerating voluntary senior-to-family housing turnover is the least-painful lever available to sustain RVC's school infrastructure** — cheaper than overrides, gentler than cuts, and it requires no one to be taxed out.

## 2. What the fiscal mechanics actually are

Three mechanics are commonly gotten wrong, including in this project's own early drafts (each was corrected in the June 2026 validation pass):

- **Exemptions shift, they don't drain.** NY school districts levy a fixed dollar amount; every exemption moves burden onto non-exempt taxpayers rather than reducing district revenue ([Comptroller's exemption brief](https://www.osc.ny.gov/files/local-government/publications/pdf/propertytax_exemptions_0.pdf)). There is no "$6.9M revenue gap" from senior exemptions — early versions of this analysis said so, and that framing was wrong.
- **STAR is Albany's money, not the district's.** STAR reimbursements are a state charge ([RPTL §1306-a](https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/laws/RPT/1306-A)). When an Enhanced STAR household sells to a young family on Basic STAR, **New York State** saves ≈ **$2,058/yr** ($3,147.01 Enhanced credit → $1,089 Basic, [2025 final amounts for RVC](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pit/property/star/comparison/28-nassau.htm)).
- **Turnover's district upside is real but indirect:** buyer renovations grow the levy via the tax-base growth factor; enrollment-linked aid follows new students; and each ~$820K sale ([ACS median](https://data.census.gov/table/ACSDT5Y2023.B25077?g=1600000US3663264)) pays the state ≈$3,280 in [transfer tax](https://www.tax.ny.gov/bus/transfer/rptidx.htm).

**Why seniors stay put.** The frictions are identifiable and mostly removable: the federal capital-gains exclusion ($250K/$500K) unindexed since 1997 — ~1.9M senior-owned homes now sit above it ([AEI](https://www.aei.org/articles/capital-gain-regulations-on-home-sales-and-baby-boomer-lock-in/)); benefit-timing friction in the year of a move; and misinformation — Enhanced STAR follows an eligible owner to a new NY home, and as of 2026 the state [auto-upgrades seniors at 65 and counts only resident-owners' income](https://www.tax.ny.gov/star/changes/) (Enhanced income limit now [$110,750](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pit/property/star/eligibility.htm)). Research: age-based exemptions measurably increase seniors' retention in place ([NBER w25468](https://www.nber.org/papers/w25468)); higher carrying costs shift large homes toward younger families ([Minneapolis Fed, 2024](https://www.minneapolisfed.org/article/2024/how-higher-property-taxes-increase-home-affordability)).

## 3. A decision is already on your calendar: §467

Two recent state changes land directly on RVC's local boards:

1. **The 65% tier exists now.** [Ch. 581 of 2025](https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/bills/2025/A3698/amendment/A) (signed Dec 5, 2025; taxable years from Jan 1, 2026) lets each taxing jurisdiction raise the senior-exemption maximum from 50% to 65% for the lowest-income tier ([current tier tables](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pit/property/exemption/seniorexempt.htm); [Nassau brochure](https://www.nassaucountyny.gov/DocumentCenter/View/49404/)).
2. **The income ceiling may rise to $75,000 on July 1, 2027.** The statute text of [RPTL §467(3)(a)](https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/laws/RPT/467) raises the maximum income limit localities *may* adopt from $50,000 to $75,000 on that date — local opt-in, ~11 months out.

Both are humane in intent. Both also deepen lock-in (the NBER finding above) and shift burden to every non-exempt homeowner — in a village whose tax rate just rose 7.2%. **The data to price these decisions does not exist in public.** Nobody outside the Assessment Department can currently say how many RVC parcels receive §467 by tier, or what an opt-in would shift onto other taxpayers.

## 4. The asks

**Village of Rockville Centre (trustees):**
- Before any §467 expansion (65% tier now, $75K ceiling in 2027), publish a **parcel-level fiscal note**: parcels by tier, dollars shifted, per-household impact on non-exempt taxpayers.
- Pair any exemption expansion with mobility measures: a downsizing information program (the 2026 STAR changes above are genuinely good news for seniors and are under-communicated), and zoning/ADU steps that create somewhere to downsize *to*.

**Nassau County (legislators):**
- Direct the [Department of Assessment](https://www.nassaucountyny.gov/1510/Assessment-Review-Commission) to publish §467 and Enhanced STAR parcel counts by school district and tier — the transparency that every downstream fiscal note depends on. (A FOIL request for RVC's counts is pending.)
- Same fiscal-note discipline county-side before adopting the $75K ceiling.

**State (Sen. Patricia Canzoneri-Fitzpatrick, SD-9; Asm. Judy Griffin, AD-21):**
- Reintroduce and floor-vote [S3309](https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/bills/2025/S3309)/[A5288](https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/bills/2025/A5288) — Enhanced STAR continuity for seniors who buy after the levy date. It cleared Aging 7-0 in April 2026 and died in Finance at session's end. It costs almost nothing and removes a genuine move-year penalty.
- Commission a fiscal note on a NY **senior property-tax deferral** enabling act (Oregon/Massachusetts/Washington/Texas models): relief without lock-in, repaid at sale.

**Federal (Rep. Laura Gillen, NY-4):**
- Cosponsor [H.R. 1340, the More Homes on the Market Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/1340) — doubles the §121 exclusion and indexes it. 146 cosponsors including LI-area members of both parties (Suozzi, Malliotakis); a bipartisan [Senate companion, S. 3332](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/3332), is endorsed by [NAR and AARP](https://www.cornyn.senate.gov/news/cornyn-bennet-colleagues-introduce-bill-to-increase-housing-availability-and-affordability/).

## 5. What this brief does not claim

No aggregate "savings recaptured" or "exemption gap" dollar figure appears here, because the parcel-level data to compute one honestly is not yet public (see §4). Assessment context: Nassau's roll has been carried forward since the 2020-21 reassessment; the 2026/27 tentative roll published Jan 2, 2026 at a Class 1 level of assessment of 0.1% ([county FAQ](https://www.nassaucountyny.gov/1517/Frequently-Asked-Questions)) — a county-wide issue noted for completeness, not a village decision.

**Phase 5 — Comparative fragmentation / "start fresh" research**
- 3 parallel researchers tested the patchwork-efficiency question against a controlled fragmentation gradient: Bergen NJ (~70 districts), Nassau NY (56), Westchester NY (~40), Montgomery MD (1), Fairfax VA (1).
- NCES admin $/pupil: Montgomery MD ~$968, Fairfax VA ~$1,408, Nassau ~$2,800 — Nassau spends ~2.9x Montgomery on administration (~$1,900/pupil, ~$370M/yr total).
- But Nassau avg ~3,700 pupils/district is already inside the literature's ~2,000-4,000 optimal band — full merger overshoots into diseconomy. Net savings after offsets (union leveling-up eats 30-60% of gross, lost BOCES aid): ~1-3% (~$185-560/household/yr).
- Risk-adjusted winner: mandated BOCES "virtual consolidation" (~$120-200M/yr, 2-4yr payback), not a redraw.
- Memphis merger case: 2013 merger booked no real savings, stranded $1B+ retiree-benefit liability, fractured within 14 months when six wealthier suburbs seceded.

## What worked (and why)

- **Fan-out sub-agents for validation domains** (coalition claims, political/legislative, school finance, STAR/§467 exemptions, demographics, calculator macros): each ran independently, wrote findings to `/tmp/rvc-extract/findings/<domain>.md`, returned with structured verdicts. This kept the main context narrow and let the agents do deep web research in parallel without blowing context limits.
- **Adversarial orchestration via `gov_brief_workflow.js`**: running 4 distinct-lens adversaries (fiscal-skeptic, political-realist, equity/desegregation-critic, homeowner-bottom-line) against a lead strategist's ranking produced genuinely useful overturns — not just polish. The JS script needed to avoid nested template literals (the runner's parser choked on `${...}` interpolations inside `.map()` arrow) — fixed by writing to a file and validating with `node` first.
- **Chrome headless PDF rendering** (`--headless --disable-gpu --no-pdf-header-footer --print-to-pdf`) was reliable for all HTML-to-PDF needs. Use Chrome, not Keynote.
- **Editorial DS from jeffpinto-site**: extracting the design tokens first before any HTML build kept visual consistency without guessing. Tokens live in `~/Projects/jeffpinto-site`.
- **Keeping governance as a separate document stream** once Jeff flagged it: prevented scope creep into the school-budget brief and deck.
- **2021 Northport-East Northport closure** was a strong, recent replacement for the 1976 Levittown reference — sourced from voices library research and directly comparable to the Long Beach 2025 near-miss.
- **Fragmentation gradient comparison** (Bergen/Nassau/Westchester/Montgomery/Fairfax) is the strongest quantitative proof in the project: same NCES metric computed identically across states, highlighted Nassau at 2.9x Montgomery.

## What didn't / dead ends

- **Keynote PDF export via AppleScript**: repeatedly failed because a third-party "Keynote Creator Studio" app intercepted AppleEvents. Workaround that eventually worked: hard-kill the process (`kill -9 <pid>`), wait for full reset, then use the simplest single-form AppleScript (no complex chaining). But the overall approach is fragile — use Chrome headless for PDF exports going forward.
- **`openpyxl` not in system Python**: had to create a throwaway venv at `/tmp/rvc-venv`. The fix is already applied but if the venv is gone, recreate: `python3 -m venv /tmp/rvc-venv && /tmp/rvc-venv/bin/pip install openpyxl python-pptx pypdfium2 markdown`.
- **5-65% bare range on the "Follow one home" infographic**: initially presented as a raw statutory range with no unit, which Jeff flagged as unreadable. Replaced everywhere with the dollar-led presentation: "~$4,700/yr" as the anchor (50% tier on a ~$9.4K school bill), with the range explained as "a sliding scale by income — 65% (≤$47K) down to 5% ($58.4K ceiling)."
- **The 1976 Levittown closure reference**: the validation agent found Pintail Lane (1976) and Cherrywood (mid-1970s) are nearly 50 years old, and the "$233.8M budget" figure was stale FY~2021 data vs the actual $274.1M (2025-26). Jeff correctly called it out as too dated. Replaced with Northport 2021.
- **Nested template literals in JS workflow script**: running the adversarial orchestration inline failed with a parse error. Fix was to write the script to a file first (`gov_brief_workflow.js`) and validate with `node` before executing.

## Key decisions

- **Two briefs on governance, not one**: the adjudicator confirmed a genuine tie — the dollar-maximization path (administrative efficiency) and the equity/desegregation path are genuinely orthogonal (the levers that cut your bill and the levers that erase racism are largely different). Both briefs were marked DRAFT FOR REVIEW.
- **"Virtual consolidation" (BOCES back-office) is the risk-adjusted winner** for cost savings, not a full district merger. Full merger overshoots the optimal scale band, and the Memphis/Louisville precedents show it rarely delivers projected savings.
- **For an RVC homeowner specifically**: the biggest dependable dollars are personal/administrative (STAR, §467, grieving the frozen assessment roll), not structural reform. The village already self-provides sanitation/police/water/electric, so it's insulated from the worst special-district waste — but also can't benefit from those consolidation plays.
- **Political voice vs. financial slice**: scale buys a seat at the governing table, but not a better per-pupil deal. The decisive catch for RVC/Locust Valley specifically: in a consolidated Nassau district, they'd be a <1% minority share of enrollment, losing 100% local control — which is exactly why wealthy small districts resist consolidation.
- **Corrections live at `/validation.html` only**: the deck/brief shows final validated numbers and sources; the delta from old to new is not surfaced in user-facing materials.
- **Governance as a separate stream**: kept separate from the school-budget brief and deck to prevent scope overload. The district-lines row in the school-budget brief stays unlinked to the governance doc.

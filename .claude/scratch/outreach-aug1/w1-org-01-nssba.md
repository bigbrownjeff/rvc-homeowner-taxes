Dear Mr. Vecchio and the Nassau-Suffolk School Boards Association,

I am a homeowner in Rockville Centre. This is a courtesy note before a nonpartisan site I built goes public on August 1: rvc-taxes.jeffpinto.com. I wanted your association to see it first, because it sits squarely on the enrollment and levy problem your members have been describing for years.

The site starts from one district's numbers and connects them to a regional pattern your association already names in public. Rockville Centre passed its 2026-27 budget at $141,323,369 on May 19, cutting 22.2 teaching positions and 40 teaching-assistant positions to stay under the tax cap. Enrollment here has fallen from 3,533 students in 2015-16 to 3,276. At the same time, 2,076 of the district's owner-occupied homes, about 27.9 percent, have a homeowner aged 65 or older. The argument is that voluntary senior-to-family housing turnover is the least painful way to steady both enrollment and the levy, since a larger home that opens up tends to return school-age children to the rolls. Every figure links to its primary public source.

The reason I think it may interest your members: the site does not ask boards to cut services or chase growth. It asks for two things boards can act on. First, before any district opts into the coming Section 467 senior-exemption expansion, whose income limit rises to $75,000 on July 1, 2027, publish a parcel-level fiscal note so trustees can see what each option shifts onto other taxpayers. Second, support the low-cost state bills that let a senior who wants to downsize move without losing an exemption mid-year, S3309 and its Assembly companion A5288. A guiding rule throughout is that any senior who never moves is left exactly as they are today.

If this is something your association could stand behind, I would welcome that. Reply if you are open to it and I will send a short paragraph you could approve for a "Backed by" note on the site. Nothing would be published without your written sign-off, and I would be glad to walk you or your staff through the one-page deck first.

Thank you for your work on behalf of Long Island's boards.

Jeff Pinto

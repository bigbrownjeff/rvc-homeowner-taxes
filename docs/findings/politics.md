## 8. NYS S5175A — RPTL §467 max exemption to 65% — VERDICT: CONFIRMED (status: now law)
- Does exactly what's claimed: local option to raise the maximum RPTL §467 senior (65+) low-income exemption above the 50% cap in place since 1966, via an income-tiered sliding scale up to 65% (50/55/60/65% tiers keyed to income below the locality's max eligibility level, M, M−$1,000, M−$2,000, M−$3,000).
- Status: Passed Senate 59-0 on June 11, 2025 (substituted by Assembly companion A3698A); delivered Dec 1, 2025; **signed by the Governor Dec 5, 2025 — Chapter 581 of 2025**; applies to taxable years beginning on/after Jan 1, 2026. As of June 2026 it is enacted law — localities (incl. Nassau munis/school districts) may now opt in.
- Sources: https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/bills/2025/S5175/amendment/A ; https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/bills/2025/A3698/amendment/A

## 9. Current NY bills on portability / senior downsizing / §467 reform — VERDICT: CONFIRMED (citable bills exist)
1. **S5175A / A3698A** — §467 max exemption 50%→65% local option. Now law (Ch. 581 of 2025) — cite as enacted, not pending.
2. **S3309 (Sen. Palumbo, R-SD1) / A5288** — exemption "portability" when seniors move: lets a senior who buys a home after the taxable status date / tax levy apply within 30 days of title transfer to keep the Enhanced STAR / senior exemption that year (fixes losing the break in a move year — directly relevant to downsizing friction). Status: reported from Aging (7-0) and recommitted to Senate Finance, Apr 21, 2026 — pending, not passed.
3. **S3574** — requires assessors to send a second §467 renewal application/notice 30 days before taxable status date (anti-lapse protection for seniors). Pending.
- No NY bill found that does California-style assessed-value portability (NY's assessment system makes that largely moot outside NYC/Nassau's class system; the "portability" angle in NY is exemption continuity, per S3309).
- Sources: https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/bills/2025/S3309 ; https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/bills/2025/A5288 ; https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/bills/2025/S3574 ; https://www.tax.ny.gov/pdf/publications/orpts/legis/legsum25.pdf

## 10. California Prop 19 portability + usage data — VERDICT: CONFIRMED (portability) / UNVERIFIED (usage stats)
- CONFIRMED: Prop 19 (approved Nov 3, 2020; effective Apr 1, 2021) lets homeowners 55+ (or severely disabled, or wildfire/disaster victims) transfer their taxable (base-year) value to a replacement primary residence **anywhere in California, up to 3 times** (vs. once, same/reciprocal county under Props 60/90). Value above an adjusted threshold (100%/105%/110% depending on timing; inflation-adjusted caps: $1,022,600 for 2/16/23–2/15/25; $1,044,586 for 2/16/25–2/15/27) is added to the transferred base. Claim filed with the county assessor within 3 years.
- USAGE DATA: No official statewide claim-count publication found from the BOE or LAO — the BOE Prop 19 page and Pub 801 are guidance only, and the LAO ballot analyses contain no post-enactment usage tallies. Closest official context: LAO's 2018 Prop 5 analysis — ~85,000 homeowners over 55 move each year in CA. County assessors hold claim-level data; any "X seniors used it" figure should be sourced to a specific county assessor or news analysis, not BOE/LAO. Recommend not citing a statewide usage number.
- Sources: https://www.boe.ca.gov/prop19/ ; https://www.boe.ca.gov/pdf/pub801.pdf ; https://lao.ca.gov/BallotAnalysis/Proposition?number=19&year=2020 ; https://lao.ca.gov/BallotAnalysis/Proposition?number=5&year=2018

## 11. Federal §121 exclusion expansion bills — VERDICT: CONFIRMED (bills exist); Gillen is NOT a cosponsor
- **H.R. 1340 — More Homes on the Market Act** (119th Congress), lead sponsors Rep. Jimmy Panetta (D-CA) + Rep. Mike Kelly (R-PA): doubles the §121 home-sale capital-gains exclusion to $500K single / $1M joint and indexes it to inflation. Status: referred to Ways & Means, still in committee as of June 2026. Cosponsors: 93 (incl. 35 R) as of late Dec 2025 per Tax Notes; ~117–123 by mid-2026 per GovTrack/QuiverQuant.
- **S. 3332** — Senate companion, introduced early Dec 2025 by Sens. John Cornyn (R-TX), Michael Bennet (D-CO), Adam Schiff (D-CA) et al. In Senate Finance.
- **Gillen**: not listed among H.R. 1340's cosponsors (QuiverQuant full roster check, June 2026; congress.gov blocked direct fetch — re-verify at https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/1340/cosponsors before publishing). Her signature housing-tax issue is SALT, not §121.
- Context: Trump publicly floated eliminating capital-gains tax on home sales (2025), keeping the issue live.
- Sources: https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/1340 ; https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/3332/all-info ; https://www.taxnotes.com/featured-news/two-bills-and-maybe-trump-favor-raising-tax-cap-home-sales/2025/12/29/7tdn1 ; https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/04/capital-gains-taxes-home-sales.html ; https://www.quiverquant.com/bills/119/hr-1340

## 14. Hempstead 18% town tax cut for 2026 — VERDICT: CONFIRMED (with a major caveat)

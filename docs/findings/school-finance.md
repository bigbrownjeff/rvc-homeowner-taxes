# Fact-Check: Rockville Centre UFSD School Finance Claims

Verified 2026-06-09 against LI Herald, Patch, NYSED (data.nysed.gov), district BoardDocs PDFs, rvcschools.org, gcnews.com, and marybethjoyce1.substack.com. LI Herald blocks automated fetchers (403); articles were retrieved with a browser user-agent and full text extracted.

---

## 1. 2025-26 approved budget = $139.89M (approved May 2025) — VERDICT: CONFIRMED

- Actual: **$139,889,187** proposed/approved; passed May 20, 2025, 1,955–970.
- Primary: district's own appropriation detail PDF, "Budgeting Appropriation Status Report For 2025-2026 BUDGET — Grand Totals: 139,889,187.00" — [BoardDocs proposed_budget_4-10-25.pdf](https://go.boarddocs.com/ny/rockville/Board.nsf/files/DFERSB6F5E4B/$file/proposed_budget_4-10-25.pdf) (dated 04/04/2025; also shows 2024-25 budget $137,641,897.62 and 2023-24 actual expenditures $133,515,658.35).
- News: "The $139.89 million spending plan is 1.63 percent larger than the current budget" — [LI Herald, "Rockville Centre 2025-2026 school budget passes…", May 20, 2025](https://www.liherald.com/valleystream/stories/rvc-2025-2026-school-budget-passed,215105).

## 2. 2025-26 levy = $109.24M; +2.6% YoY — VERDICT: CONFIRMED

- Levy: **$109,240,000** — "The district's projected tax levy for the coming year is $111,911,163, up from $109,240,000 in the 2025-26 year." — [Patch, "RVC Schools Facing $3.78M Budget Deficit For 26-27", Jan 30, 2026](https://patch.com/new-york/rockvillecentre/rvc-schools-facing-3-78m-budget-deficit-26-27-district-officials-say).
- Increase: "includes a 2.6 percent tax levy increase, which is just under the district's allowable tax cap of 2.62 percent" — [LI Herald, May 20, 2025](https://www.liherald.com/valleystream/stories/rvc-2025-2026-school-budget-passed,215105); same language in the [May 16, 2025 proposal article](https://www.liherald.com/merrick/stories/rvc-2025-school-budget-proposal,214994).

## 3. Projected 2026-27 deficit = $3.78M; cited LI Herald article exists — VERDICT: CONFIRMED

- The cited article exists at the slug given (liherald serves it under multiple section paths): [LI Herald, "Rockville Centre school district projects $3.78M budget deficit for 2026-27", posted Friday, Feb 6, 2026, by Brian Kacharaba](https://www.liherald.com/baldwin/stories/rockville-centre-school-district-projects-378m-budget-deficit-for-2026-27,220209).
- Quote: "The Rockville Centre School District is facing a projected $3.78 million budget deficit in 2026-27, district officials said at a Jan. 29 Board of Education work session…"
- IMPORTANT UPDATE: the gap was closed; the board adopted a **$141,323,369** 2026-27 budget on Apr 16, 2026 (+1.03% spending, −22.2 teaching positions, −40 TA positions), and voters passed it **1,915–1,195 on May 19, 2026** ([LI Herald adoption article](https://www.liherald.com/rockvillecentre/stories/rockville-centre-school-board-adopts-1413m-budget-preserves-tax-cap,221736); [LI Herald vote-results article](https://www.liherald.com/stories/school-budget-passes-in-rockville-centre,222477); [Patch, Apr 17, 2026](https://patch.com/new-york/rockvillecentre/rockville-centre-school-district-moves-ahead-141-3m-budget-proposal)). Any document still treating the $3.78M deficit as open is OUTDATED.

## 4. Allowable 2026-27 levy = $111,911,163 (+2.45%) vs sibling claim of 2.1% cap — VERDICT: OUTDATED (2.45% was the January projection; final ≈2.1%, adopted +2.06%)

- $111,911,163 / +2.45% is real but was the district's **January 2026 projection**: "Rehak projected the district's allowable tax levy for 2026-27 at just under $112 million, a 2.45 percent increase" ([LI Herald, Feb 6, 2026](https://www.liherald.com/baldwin/stories/rockville-centre-school-district-projects-378m-budget-deficit-for-2026-27,220209)); exact dollar figure in [Patch, Jan 30, 2026](https://patch.com/new-york/rockvillecentre/rvc-schools-facing-3-78m-budget-deficit-26-27-district-officials-say). Arithmetic checks: 111,911,163 / 109,240,000 = +2.445%.
- The final cap came in lower: the Joyce substack (Mar 18, 2026, after the Mar 1 comptroller tax-cap filing) says the levy cap is "about 2.1% this year"; the adopted budget "carries a 2.06% tax levy increase, or roughly $2.25 million, while remaining under the state-imposed cap" ([LI Herald, Apr 16, 2026](https://www.liherald.com/rockvillecentre/stories/rockville-centre-school-board-adopts-1413m-budget-preserves-tax-cap,221736)).
- Resolution: **the sibling document's ~2.1% is the operative 2026-27 number; 2.45%/$111.9M is the superseded early projection.** (Adopted levy ≈ $109.24M × 1.0206 ≈ $111.49M.) Background: OSC capped the base inflation factor at 2% statewide for 2026-27 fiscal years ([LI Herald, Jan 30, 2026](https://www.liherald.com/stories/rockville-centre-schools-brace-for-challenging-202627-budget,220043), quoting DiNapoli; inflation factor 2.63% → 2% limit applies).

## 5. State aid ≈ $22M (~15.7% of budget) — VERDICT: UNVERIFIED at that precision; likely overstated by ~$1-2M

- No public source found stating $22M. Closest public figures:
  - 2024-25: state aid "estimated to increase 2.6 percent to $19.8 million" (LI Herald coverage of the 2024-25 budget, ["Rockville Centre schools float $137.6M spending plan"](https://www.liherald.com/seaford/stories/rockville-centre-schools-float-1376m-spending-plan,207242), March 2024).
  - 2025-26: foundation aid +2%, no change to expense-based aids ([LI Herald, May 16, 2025](https://www.liherald.com/merrick/stories/rvc-2025-school-budget-proposal,214994)) → implies ≈$20.2-20.4M total.
  - Substack (Mar 18, 2026): state aid is "15% of the budget and it is only increasing 1%" → 15% of ~$135-141M ≈ $20-21M.
  - Patch (Apr 17, 2026): a 1-percentage-point foundation-aid swing ≈ $125,000, implying foundation aid ≈ $12.5M (the rest is building/BOCES/transportation aid).
- $22M = 15.7% of $139.89M is arithmetically self-consistent but not corroborated; best public estimate is **~$20-21M (~15%)**.

Same article also notes special-ed share of budget fell from ~15% (2019) to ~13.5% (2025) per the same speaker.

## 8. Unrestricted reserves $8M = "8% of expenditures"; peer avg 14%; Garden City $27M on ~$122.7M budget — VERDICT: CLOSE for the substack-sourced figures; WRONG on GC's budget being $122.7M

- Substack (Mar 18, 2026) does say: RVC has "$8m unrestricted cash to operate a budget of ~$135m", a ratio she gives as "8%", vs peer average "14%", and Garden City "$27m in unrestricted cash" on a similar-size budget. So the claim faithfully reproduces its source. Caveat: $8M is ~5.7-6% of a $135-140M budget, so her own "8%" label doesn't reconcile cleanly with the $8M/$135M pair — denominator basis unclear.
- Garden City's actual budgets: **$137.8M adopted for 2025-26** (+2.82%, levy +2.42%) — [Garden City News, "BOE adopts proposed 2025-26 budget"](https://www.gcnews.com/articles/boe-adopts-proposed-2025-26-budget/); **~$142.4M proposed for 2026-27** — [Garden City News, "District proposes $142 mill budget…"](https://www.gcnews.com/articles/district-proposes-142-mill-budget-faces-costly-mandate/). The claimed "$122.7M" matches no recent GC budget; it is almost certainly GC's **2023-24 actual expenditures from the ST-3** (the substack's data vintage: $32.7K/pupil × ~3,750 students ≈ $122.7M) — i.e., actual spend, mislabeled as "budget."
- GC's $27M unrestricted reserves: no independent public confirmation found (GC news coverage mentions a ~$13.6M capital reserve and a proposed new $11M capital reserve); the $27M figure exists only in the substack's analysis of audited financials. UNVERIFIED independently.

## 9. Appropriated fund balance $2.8M → ~$1.6M; unappropriated $4.8M (3.5% vs 4% max); ~$4.2M ERS+TRS reserves — VERDICT: CONFIRMED

- All from [LI Herald, Feb 6, 2026](https://www.liherald.com/baldwin/stories/rockville-centre-school-district-projects-378m-budget-deficit-for-2026-27,220209), quoting Asst. Supt. Jacqueline Rehak:

  - ERS reserve "$2.5 million" + TRS reserve "$1.65 million" = **$4.15M ≈ $4.2M**. ✓

## 10. Enrollment 3,326 (2025-26), 3,260 projected (2026-27), 3,548 in 2016 — VERDICT: CLOSE / partly UNVERIFIED

- District-stated (via Herald, Feb 6, 2026): "Total enrollment has dropped from **3,548 students in 2016** to a projected **3,260 in 2026-27**." ✓ for those two numbers as district statements.
- **3,326 for 2025-26: not found in any public source.** NYSED has not yet published 2025-26 enrollment. Possible conflation with NYSED's 2023-24 enrollment of 3,319 (see #11).
- NYSED actuals (data.nysed.gov, district K-12 enrollment, instid 800000049383):
  - 2015-16: **3,533** ([NYSED](https://data.nysed.gov/enrollment.php?year=2016&instid=800000049383))
  - 2016-17: **3,534** ([NYSED](https://data.nysed.gov/enrollment.php?year=2017&instid=800000049383)) — vs district's "3,548 in 2016" (14 off; district internal count basis differs slightly)
  - 2024-25: **3,276** ([NYSED](https://data.nysed.gov/enrollment.php?year=2025&instid=800000049383))
  - 2025-26: not yet published (year=2026 query returns 2024-25 data).
- Note: NYSED 2024-25 (3,276) is already below the claimed 2025-26 figure (3,326), so 3,326 likely uses a different count basis (e.g., includes UPK or out-placed students) or is stale.

## 11. Per-pupil $42,050/$42,060 = $139.89M ÷ 3,326; NYSED official measure — VERDICT: CLOSE on arithmetic; NYSED says $37,973

- Arithmetic: $139,889,187 ÷ 3,326 = **$42,059.6 → $42,060** ("$42,050" is a small rounding error; and the denominator 3,326 itself is unverified, see #10).
- NYSED Financial Transparency Report (official per-pupil expenditure), 2023-24, district level: **$37,972.78** on 3,319 enrolled — [data.nysed.gov expenditures page](https://data.nysed.gov/expenditures.php?year=2024&instid=800000049383).
- Three measures in circulation, all different bases: budget÷enrollment ≈ $42.1K; substack's ST-3 2023-24 actuals ≈ **$40.2K**; NYSED transparency measure $37.97K (excludes certain expenditures, different enrollment count). Budget÷enrollment overstates vs NYSED's official measure by ~$4K.

## Most important corrections

1. **Claim 4 (tax cap): the 2.45% / $111,911,163 allowable levy was a January 2026 projection that was superseded.** The final 2026-27 cap came in around 2.1%; the adopted and voter-approved budget carries a 2.06% levy increase (~$111.5M levy). The "sibling document" claiming ~2.1% is right for the final number.
2. **The $3.78M deficit story has an ending the claims omit:** a $141,323,369 budget (+1.03%) closing the gap largely through ~22 teaching, 40 TA, and other staff cuts was adopted Apr 16, 2026 and passed by voters 1,915–1,195 on May 19, 2026 — and substack author Mary Beth Joyce was elected to the board in that same vote (she was a candidate, not a trustee, when she wrote the March posts).
3. **Garden City's budget is ~$137.8M (2025-26) / ~$142.4M (2026-27 proposed), not $122.7M.** The $122.7M is almost certainly GC's 2023-24 actual ST-3 expenditures (the substack's data basis) mislabeled as its budget; the $27M GC reserve figure rests solely on the substack and is not independently confirmed.
4. **State aid of "$22M (15.7%)" is not supported by any public source found** — published figures are $19.8M for 2024-25 (LI Herald) and "15% of budget" rising 1% (substack), implying ~$20-21M.
5. **Enrollment figures need care:** NYSED K-12 actuals are 3,533 (2015-16), 3,534 (2016-17), 3,276 (2024-25); 2025-26 is not yet published, so the claimed 3,326 is unverifiable (and exceeds the 2024-25 NYSED count); "3,548 in 2016" is the district's own slightly higher count. Per-pupil "$42,060" is correct arithmetic on unofficial inputs, but NYSED's official 2023-24 per-pupil expenditure is $37,972.78.
6. **Riverside's "$15,000 more per student" and the "288 spare seats" are residents' public-comment assertions/inferences, not district analyses** — no published per-building cost study or capacity study exists.
